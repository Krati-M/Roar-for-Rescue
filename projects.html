Petition to Protect the Chitwan National Park, Nepal

Roar for Rescue is calling for support to protect the critical habitats within the Chitwan National Park in Nepal. This area is home to Bengal tigers, rhinos, and countless other endangered species. Join us in petitioning the Nepalese government to expand protections and prevent encroachment on these precious lands.

Community Awareness Program

We’re actively working to raise awareness in communities near tiger habitats about the importance of conservation and coexistence with wildlife. Our workshops, educational outreach, and community programs aim to foster a deep connection with and respect for Bengal tigers and their ecosystems.

Tiger Remains Found in Mongolia Dated to 30 Years

A recent study reveals evidence of tiger remains in Mongolia, carbon-dated to 30 years old. This discovery changes our understanding of tiger lifespans and habitats. This tiger, which they have named Raja after Raj, the previous owner of the longest lifespan award, was alive more than 500 years ago, and may have even seen the Mongol Empire! Since could mean that tigers, while they may no longer be able to, used to have longer lifespans. The causes of this decline may even be human caused since most tigers live in pollutated, shruken and even non-natural habitats. Read the article Here

Happy Birthday Tara!! Celebrating 23 Years

Tara, our oldest resident tiger, just turned 23! She celebrated her birthday with the youth outreach program at our sanctuary in Bhutan. Join us in wishing her a happy birthday! Throughout their wonderful life Tara has faced many challenges, with poachers and agriculture detroying the habaiat she once called home. This goes to remidn us that tigers are still suffereing out there and everyday, we have a chance to make sure another human isn't the cause of their discomfort. Let us be happy that Tara has found a safer and better home in Bhutan, but pray that eventually every tiger will be just as happy in the wild without human help.
